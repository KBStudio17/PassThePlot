Pass the Plot is a story game for you and four friends. Everyone writes one sentence — but you only ever see the title and the last two words written before yours. No plan. No context. No idea where it's going. Then the whole story is revealed at once.

How a story comes together

Four small turns. One story nobody fully controls.

1

Start a story

Pick a title and invite four friends. That's the whole setup.

2

Write blind

When it's your turn, you see the title and only the last two words written before you.

3

Pass it on

One sentence is the whole turn. Then it's the next writer's problem.

4

Reveal the chaos

Once all five lines are in, the full story appears at once — color-coded by author.

Made to be shared, not perfected

No one writes the whole story. That's the point.

Every reveal comes out a little stranger than anyone intended — and that's the best part. Finished stories are made for screenshots, group chats, and reading out loud. Collect borders and quills along the way to give your reveals their own look, entirely cosmetic, never a gameplay edge.
